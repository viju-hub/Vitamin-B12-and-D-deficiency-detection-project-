Vitamin D

Vitamin D is a fat-soluble vitamin essential for various bodily functions, primarily known for its role in maintaining bone health by aiding calcium absorption. It also plays a crucial role in immune function, cell growth, and reducing inflammation. The primary source of vitamin D is sunlight, as the skin synthesizes it when exposed to UVB rays.

Vitamin B12

Vitamin B12, also known as cobalamin, is a water-soluble vitamin essential for various bodily functions. It plays a critical role in DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, neurological function, and metabolism. The body cannot produce vitamin B12 on its own, so it must be obtained from dietary sources such as meat, fish, eggs, dairy products, and fortified foods, or through supplements.
